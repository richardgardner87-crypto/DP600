# GCC Supplement Market Commodity Codes & Export Policies

## Key HS Commodity Codes for Supplements

### Vitamins & Minerals
- **2936.90** - Other organic compounds (vitamins, provitamins, etc.)
- **2106.90** - Food preparations & dietary supplements
- **2842** - Salts of inorganic acids & peroxides (mineral supplements)

### Herbal Supplements & Plant-Based
- **1211.90** - Plants & plant parts used medicinally
- **1302** - Vegetable saps & extracts (herbal extracts)
- **3004** - Medicaments consisting of mixed ingredients
- **3005** - Medicaments containing antibiotics/hormones

### Finished Supplement Products
- **3004.90** - Medicaments (other) - for retail sale
- **3005.90** - Other medicaments for retail

### Amino Acids & Proteins
- **2922.41** - Amino acids (lysine, glutamic acid, etc.)
- **3502** - Albumins, albuminates & other derivatives
- **3505** - Dextrins & starch derivatives

## GCC Country-Specific Export Policies

### **Saudi Arabia**
**Regulatory Body:** SFDA (Saudi Food & Drug Authority)

**Key Requirements:**
- Requires SFDA approval for supplement registration
- Import/Export license mandatory
- Health claims must be substantiated
- Banned ingredients: Khat, ephedra derivatives
- Maximum caffeine: 200mg per unit for energy supplements
- Food-grade GMO labeling required
- Halal certification preferred but not mandatory for non-meat items

**Documents Required:**
- Certificate of Analysis
- Safety data sheet
- Manufacturing facility registration
- Product composition details

---

### **UAE** (Dubai/Abu Dhabi)
**Regulatory Body:** FMCG Authority, MOEHE (Ministry of Environment)

**Key Requirements:**
- UAE.S standards compliance for supplements
- GCC Common Tariff applies (5-30% depending on product type)
- Allergen labeling mandatory
- Restricted ingredients: Many synthetic additives, certain colorants
- Import permit from Customs Authority
- Product registration with trade body

**Special Rules:**
- Cannabis-derived compounds: **BANNED**
- Controlled herbs (Ephedra, Kava): Restricted
- Nutritional labeling must be in Arabic
- Alcohol content: Max 0.5%

---

### **Kuwait**
**Regulatory Body:** Public Authority for Food Safety

**Key Requirements:**
- Import license mandatory
- Certificate of Free Sale from origin country
- Laboratory testing certificates required
- Banned substances list strictly enforced
- Shelf-life documentation required

---

### **Qatar**
**Regulatory Body:** Qatar Food Safety Authority

**Key Requirements:**
- Halal certification highly preferred
- Import permits from Ministry of Commerce
- Registration with local distributor required
- Label requirements: Arabic translation mandatory
- Nutrient content verification

---

### **Bahrain**
**Regulatory Body:** MOH (Ministry of Health)

**Key Requirements:**
- Registration with MOH for market entry
- Favorable regulations for conventional supplements
- CE marking acceptable (EU supplements)
- Import permit simple process
- Halal certification important for retail

---

### **Oman**
**Regulatory Body:** Ministry of Health

**Key Requirements:**
- Similar to Saudi requirements
- Banned substances: Ephedrine, certain stimulants
- Local importer registration necessary
- Documentation in English or Arabic

---

## Common GCC-Wide Restrictions

### **Banned/Heavily Restricted Ingredients**
- ❌ Ephedrine & ephedra alkaloids
- ❌ DMAA (Dimethylamylamine)
- ❌ Certain anabolic compounds
- ❌ Prescription drug ingredients
- ❌ Kava kava (hepatotoxicity concerns)
- ❌ Unproven traditional ingredients without documentation
- ⚠️ Caffeine (limits: varies 200-400mg per serving)
- ⚠️ Yohimbe (restricted in some emirates)

### **Mandatory Labeling** (all GCC)
- Product name & ingredients (Arabic + English)
- Nutrient content per serving
- Allergen statements
- Shelf-life/Expiration date
- Manufacturing country
- Batch/Lot number
- Storage instructions
- Health warnings (if applicable)

### **Tariff Classification**
GCC Common External Tariff (GCCT):
- **HS 2106.90** (Dietary supplements): 5%
- **HS 3004** (Medicaments): 0-5%
- **HS 2936.90** (Vitamins): 5%
- **HS 1211** (Medicinal plants): 5%

## Export Documentation Checklist

### Required for GCC Import:
1. ✅ Certificate of Analysis (lab test results)
2. ✅ Certificate of Free Sale (country of origin)
3. ✅ Halal certificate (if marketed as halal)
4. ✅ Manufacturing facility inspection report
5. ✅ Product registration forms (country-specific)
6. ✅ Insurance certificate (liability)
7. ✅ Packing list & commercial invoice
8. ✅ Bill of lading/Airway bill
9. ✅ Incoterms agreement
10. ✅ Health & safety declaration

## Export Processing Timelines

- **Document verification:** 5-10 days
- **Customs clearance:** 2-5 days
- **Laboratory testing:** 3-7 days
- **Total typical process:** 10-22 days

**Expedited routes available** through licensed brokers (adds 15-20% to cost)

---

## Compliance Strategy for Supplement Exports to GCC

1. **Pre-Export Phase**
   - Verify ingredient compliance against SFDA/MOEHE banned list
   - Obtain international certifications (ISO 9001, GMP)
   - Get halal certification

2. **Documentation Phase**
   - Complete country-specific registration forms
   - Obtain CoA from accredited lab
   - Get Certificate of Free Sale from origin authority

3. **Shipping Phase**
   - Use GCC-approved customs broker
   - Include all required labeling in Arabic
   - Ship via established routes (minimal delay risk)

4. **Post-Import Phase**
   - Local distributor handles retail registration
   - Ongoing compliance monitoring for ingredient changes
